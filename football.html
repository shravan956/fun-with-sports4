What is Football?

Football (also known as soccer in some countries) is a sport played between two teams of eleven players each. The objective is to score by getting a ball into the opposing team's goal.

Learn the rules and gameplay fundamentals below.

The Football Field

The field is rectangular, typically 100-110 meters long and 64-75 meters wide. It is divided into two halves, with a goal at each end. The center spot is where the game begins.

This is the center of the field, where play starts!

Basic Football Rules

  • Duration: A match consists of two 45-minute halves, with a 15-minute break.
  • Offside: A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent’s goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them.
  • Free Kicks: Free kicks are awarded for fouls. They can be direct (allowing a shot at goal) or indirect (must touch another player before scoring).
  • Throw-ins: When the ball crosses the touchline, the team that didn’t touch it last gets possession. The ball is thrown back into play from the point where it went out.

Football Positions

Goalkeeper

Protects the goal and prevents the opposition from scoring.

Defenders

Helps prevent the opposing team from scoring. Includes center-backs and full-backs.

Midfielders

Links the defense and the attack, controlling the pace and direction of play.

Forwards

The main attackers, whose job is to score goals.

Fouls and Offenses

Common fouls in football include:

  • Tripping: A player trips an opponent intentionally.
  • Handball: A player deliberately handles the ball (except the goalkeeper within their own box).
  • Dangerous Play: Actions like high kicks or playing the ball aggressively can result in fouls.

Scoring in Football

To score a goal, the ball must completely cross the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar.

The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins. In case of a tie, extra time or penalty kicks may be used.
