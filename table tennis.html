About Table Tennis

Table Tennis, also known as Ping Pong, is a fast-paced indoor sport played between two or four players. The objective is to score points by hitting a lightweight ball over a net onto the opponent's side of the table using a small racket. It is played at both recreational and competitive levels and is one of the most popular indoor sports worldwide.

Rules & Regulations

  • Objective: Score points by hitting the ball over the net and into the opponent's side of the table.
  • Match Duration: A game is typically played to 11 points, with players switching sides after each game. Matches can be best of 3, 5, or 7 games.
  • Serving: The serve must start from behind the end line, and the ball must bounce once on the server's side and once on the opponent's side.
  • Scoring: A point is won when the opponent fails to return the ball within the table's boundaries or commits a fault.
  • Faults: A fault is committed if the ball doesn't bounce on the opponent's side, or if the player touches the table with their free hand.
  • Rackets: Players use paddles (rackets) covered with rubber. Both sides can be used for different strokes, usually one for forehand and the other for backhand.
  • Let Serve: If the ball touches the net but lands in the correct service area, the serve is replayed.
  • Double Matches: In doubles, players alternate hitting the ball, and the serve must be made diagonally from the right side.
