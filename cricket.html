What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps.

Cricket Rules

  • 1. The Game: Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each.
  • 2. The Toss: The game begins with a coin toss to decide which team bats first.
  • 3. The Batting: The team that bats must attempt to score as many runs as possible.
  • 4. The Bowling: The opposing team bowls to dismiss the batsmen and prevent runs.
  • 5. Dismissals: There are several ways a batsman can be dismissed, such as being bowled, caught, or run out.
  • 6. Overs: Each bowler bowls six balls in an over, after which the bowler is changed.

Cricket Regulations

Cricket is governed by a set of regulations, including laws provided by the International Cricket Council (ICC). Some of the key regulations are:

  • 1. Playing Time: Matches are played within specified time frames, ranging from T20 (20 overs) to Test matches (5 days).
  • 2. Umpires: Two umpires oversee the match, ensuring that the rules are followed.
  • 3. Code of Conduct: Players must adhere to a strict code of conduct regarding behavior, discipline, and respect for others.
  • 4. Helmet Rules: Batsmen must wear helmets when facing fast bowlers for safety.
