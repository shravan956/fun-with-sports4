About Basketball

Basketball is a team sport played between two teams of five players on a rectangular court. The objective is to score by shooting a ball through the hoop, a netted structure 10 feet high. It is one of the most popular sports worldwide, played both professionally and recreationally.

Rules & Regulations

  • Objective: Score points by shooting the ball into the opponent's hoop.
  • Game Duration: A standard game consists of 4 quarters, each lasting 12 minutes (NBA). The game may go into overtime if scores are tied.
  • Dribbling: Players must dribble the ball while moving. Walking or running without dribbling is considered traveling.
  • Fouls: Personal fouls are given for illegal physical contact. Each player can accumulate up to 6 fouls in an NBA game.
  • Positions: Players can assume positions such as point guard, shooting guard, small forward, power forward, and center, each with specific responsibilities.
  • Offense & Defense: The team with the ball is on offense, while the opposing team is on defense. Teams aim to either score or prevent scoring.
  • 3-Point Line: A basket made beyond the 3-point line is worth 3 points.
  • Shot Clock: In the NBA, a team must attempt a shot within 24 seconds of gaining possession.
